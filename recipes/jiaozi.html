Jiaozi (Chinese Dumplings)


The Symbol of Wealth and Reunion

This recipe is defined by its historical significance; Jiaozi are traditional Chinese dumplings that have been a staple for over 1,800 years. Shaped like ancient gold ingots (sycee), they are a powerful symbol of wealth and prosperity, often served during the Lunar New Year to bring good fortune.

The Art of the Juicy Filling

A critical characteristic of this dish is the tender, savory filling, typically made with a blend of ground pork and finely shredded napa cabbage. To ensure the perfect texture, the meat is stirred vigorously in one direction until it becomes sticky, allowing the aromatics like ginger and sesame oil to fully permeate the mixture.

Ingredients

The Savory Filling

  • Ground Pork: 1 lb (500g) fatty ground pork for maximum juiciness.
  • Napa Cabbage: 2 cups finely shredded and squeezed of excess moisture.
  • Aromatics: 1 tbsp minced ginger, 3 cloves minced garlic, and 2 finely chopped scallions.
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1.5 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine, and 1 tsp salt.

The Wrappers and Dipping Sauce

  • Wrappers: 1 pack of round dumpling wrappers (approximately 40â€“50 pieces).
  • Dipping Sauce Base: 2 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar.
  • Sauce Aromatics: 1 tsp chili oil and 1 clove of minced garlic.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling: Combine the ground pork, ginger, garlic, and liquid seasonings in a large bowl. Stir vigorously in one direction until the meat becomes sticky, then fold in the shredded cabbage.
  2. Fill the Wrappers: Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of a wrapper. Lightly wet the outer edges of the wrapper with water to act as an adhesive.
  3. Pleat and Seal: Fold the wrapper into a half-moon shape. Create small pleats along the top edge and pinch tightly to seal the dumpling, ensuring no air is trapped inside.
  4. Boil the Jiaozi: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and carefully drop in the dumplings. Stir gently with a spoon to prevent them from sticking to the bottom.
  5. The Water Addition Method: When the water returns to a boil, add half a cup of cold water. Repeat this process twice more; when the dumplings float to the surface after the final boil, they are ready.
  6. Assemble and Serve: Use a slotted spoon to drain the dumplings and transfer them to a platter. Serve immediately while hot alongside the prepared dipping sauce.
