Peking Duck


The Legendary Crispy Skin

This recipe is defined by its shattering crispy skin and succulent meat, a royal dish dating back to the Imperial era of China. The secret lies in the air-drying process, which separates the skin from the fat, allowing it to crisp up perfectly during roasting. It is traditionally served with thin mandarin pancakes, sweet bean sauce, and fresh aromatics.

The Art of the Glaze

A critical step in this recipe involves repeatedly bathing the duck in a maltose syrup glaze. This coating not only provides the signature deep mahogany color but also creates a thin, candy-like shell that enhances the texture of the skin. When carved, the skin is often dipped in sugar to highlight its delicate crunch and rich flavor.

Ingredients

The Duck and Glaze

  • Whole Duck: 4–5 lb (2kg) whole duck with skin intact and head/neck preferred.
  • Glaze Base: 1/2 cup maltose (or honey), 1/2 cup water, and 2 tbsp red rice vinegar.
  • Internal Seasoning: 2 tbsp Chinese five-spice powder, 2 tbsp salt, and 3 slices of ginger.
  • Aromatics: 2 whole star anise and 1 cinnamon stick.

For Serving

  • Mandarin Pancakes: 20 thin steamed flour pancakes.
  • Sauce: 1/2 cup Hoisin sauce or Sweet Bean Sauce (Tianmianjiang).
  • Vegetables: 1 cucumber (julienned) and 4 scallions (white parts only, shredded).

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Duck: Clean the duck thoroughly and pat it dry. Use a straw or air pump to carefully blow air between the skin and the meat to separate them.
  2. Scald the Skin: Pour boiling water over the entire surface of the duck until the skin tightens and turns white. Drain and pat dry again.
  3. Apply the Glaze: Bring the maltose, vinegar, and water to a simmer. Brush the glaze evenly over the skin, then let it dry in a cool, drafty place for at least 12–24 hours.
  4. Internal Seasoning: Rub the five-spice powder and salt inside the cavity. Stuff with ginger, star anise, and cinnamon before sealing the cavity with a skewer.
  5. Roast the Duck: Place the duck on a rack in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C). Roast for about 70–90 minutes, turning halfway through, until the skin is dark red and crispy.
  6. The Resting Phase: Remove the duck from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring the meat stays moist.
  7. Carving: Use a sharp knife to carefully slice the crispy skin first, then the meat into thin, bite-sized pieces.
  8. Assemble: Spread a little sauce on a pancake, add a piece of duck skin, meat, cucumber, and scallion. Fold and serve immediately.
