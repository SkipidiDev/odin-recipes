Sichuan Cusine


The Bold Mala Flavor

This recipe is defined by its "mala" flavor, a signature of Sichuan cuisine that combines the numbing sensation of Sichuan peppercorns with the fiery heat of dried red chilies. The beef is typically sliced paper-thin and "velveted" (marinated in a mixture of cornstarch and rice wine) to ensure it stays incredibly tender. It is then quickly poached in a rich, spicy broth built from a base of doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), ginger, and garlic.

The Sizzling Oil Technique

A critical final step in this recipe involves layering fresh minced garlic, dried chili flakes, and scallions over the cooked beef and vegetables. Sizzling hot oil is then poured directly over these aromatics, flash-frying them instantly to release a deep, smoky fragrance and vibrant red color. This technique creates the shimmering, oily surface seen in your image and ensures the spices permeate every bite of the dish

Ingredients

The Meat and Marinade

  • Beef: 1 lb (500g) flank steak or rib-eye, sliced very thinly against the grain.
  • Tenderizer: 1/8 tsp baking soda (optional, for "velveting").
  • Binding: 1 egg white and 1–2 tbsp cornstarch.
  • Seasoning: 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 1 tsp light soy sauce, and a pinch of salt.

The Broth Base

  • Aromatics: 1-inch piece of ginger (sliced), 3 cloves of garlic (minced), and 2 scallions.
  • The Soul: 2–3 tbsp Doubanjiang (Sichuan fermented chili bean paste).
  • Liquid: 3 cups chicken or beef stock.

The Vegetables and Toppings

  • Base Greens: 2 cups of napa cabbage, bok choy, or bean sprouts.
  • Final Sizzle: 1 tbsp dried chili flakes, 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns, 3 cloves minced garlic, and 1/4 cup neutral oil.

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Beef: Toss the thinly sliced beef with soy sauce, wine, and baking soda. After 15 minutes, mix in the egg white and cornstarch until velvety.
  2. Prepare the Vegetable Bed: Quickly stir-fry your chosen greens with a pinch of salt until wilted. Place them at the bottom of a deep serving bowl.
  3. Build the Spicy Broth: In a wok, sauté ginger, garlic, and scallions. Add the Doubanjiang and stir-fry until the oil turns red.
  4. Boil the Broth: Pour in the stock and bring to a rolling boil. Let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Poach the Beef: Reduce heat to a simmer. Carefully drop beef slices in one by one. Cook for only 60–90 seconds until just opaque.
  6. Assemble: Pour the beef and spicy broth over the vegetables in your serving bowl.
  7. The Final Sizzle: Sprinkle minced garlic, chili flakes, and ground Sichuan peppercorns on top. Heat 1/4 cup oil until shimmering.
  8. Finish: Carefully pour the hot oil directly over the spices. Garnish with fresh cilantro or scallion greens.
